# Competitor Directory Landscape — Addiction Treatment

## Overview

The addiction treatment directory market is dominated by a small number of high-traffic aggregators that charge facilities for enhanced placement. Understanding the competitive landscape lets Sonia position Sobriety Select accurately — not as a replacement for these platforms, but as a complementary channel with differentiated audience intent and transparent attribution.

## Key Competing Directories

### Psychology Today
Psychology Today's treatment center finder is one of the highest-traffic directories in the space, driven by its large therapist directory cross-sell audience. Listings range from free basic entries to paid enhanced profiles. The audience skews toward therapy-adjacent outpatient and IOP; large residential and detox facilities often find it under-indexes for their census profile. The "missing from Psychology Today" signal in our enrichment pipeline flags facilities that have not claimed or enhanced their profile — a FOMO hook for Claimed and Select tier conversations.

### Rehabs.com and Recovery.com
Rehabs.com (owned by Recovery Brands / American Addiction Centers) and Recovery.com compete on SEO-heavy directory search traffic. Both charge premium rates for top placement and have reputational baggage from the AAC ownership history in the industry. Facilities that have had negative experiences with pay-to-play directory models are often warm to Sobriety Select's transparent referral-tag attribution model. The combined "missing from Rehabs.com and Recovery.com" signal is a strong secondary FOMO hook, particularly for operators who have heard the industry complaints about those platforms.

### FindTreatment.gov (SAMHSA)
SAMHSA's national treatment locator is free and government-operated. It is not a competitor — it is a data source for our lead pipeline and a trust signal when a facility appears there. Presence on FindTreatment.gov indicates a licensed, legitimate operator and is not a selling point or objection in either direction.
