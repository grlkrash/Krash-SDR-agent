# Addiction Treatment Industry — Market Overview

## Overview

The U.S. addiction treatment industry encompasses approximately 15,000–17,000 licensed facilities ranging from single-location sober living homes to large multi-state behavioral health systems. The market is highly fragmented at the lower end (independent operators, faith-based programs, peer-run recovery homes) and increasingly consolidated at the upper end through private equity roll-ups. Understanding the ownership structure behind a facility — solo operator vs. PE-backed chain vs. hospital system — is the fastest way to predict deal size, decision speed, and the right product tier.

## Buyer Segmentation by Facility Type

Sober living operators (6–30 beds, no clinical license required in most states) are typically owner-operated with tight margins and high census sensitivity. They are the primary Claimed Listing buyer — the $600/year price point is within a single bed-night's revenue, and the verified badge addresses their core credibility gap with families searching online. IOP and PHP programs (outpatient, no residential beds) skew toward the Select tier: they have a marketing function, some digital presence, and a recognizable pain point around online lead sourcing. Residential detox and long-term residential programs (30–90+ days, 20–200+ beds) are the Premium and SEO upsell targets — they have real marketing budgets, often run Google Ads or CallRail, and their revenue per admission ($10K–$60K+) makes a $9,600/year listing trivially ROI-positive if it moves even one admission per quarter.
